Introduction to Touch Typing

Welcome to TypeFluent's beginner guide to touch typing! This comprehensive course will teach you how to type efficiently without looking at the keyboard.

What is Touch Typing?

Touch typing is the ability to type without looking at the keyboard, using muscle memory to find keys. This method allows you to:

  • Type faster (average typists reach 40+ WPM)
  • Maintain high accuracy
  • Reduce strain and fatigue
  • Focus on your content instead of hunting for keys

How This Course Works

We'll guide you through progressive lessons, starting with the home row keys and gradually expanding to cover the entire keyboard. Each lesson includes:

  • Visual guides showing correct finger placement
  • Short exercises to build muscle memory
  • Practice texts using only the keys you've learned

Proper Posture and Hand Position

Before you start typing, it's important to establish good habits with proper posture and hand positioning.

Sitting Position

  • Sit up straight with your back against the chair
  • Keep your feet flat on the floor
  • Position your screen at eye level
  • Keep your elbows at a 90-degree angle
  • Wrists should hover slightly above the keyboard, not resting on the desk

Hand Position

Your hands should be positioned with fingers curved gently over the keyboard:

  • Fingers should be curved, not flat
  • Thumbs rest on or above the spacebar
  • Start with your fingers on the home row (ASDF for left hand, JKL; for right hand)
  • Maintain a light touch - don't pound the keys

Remember: Good posture reduces fatigue and prevents repetitive strain injuries. Take breaks every 20-30 minutes!

Practice Tips & Next Steps

Congratulations on completing the basic touch typing lessons! Here are some tips to continue improving:

Daily Practice

  • Practice for at least 15-30 minutes daily
  • Focus on accuracy first, then speed
  • Use the TypeFluent training mode to practice progressively
  • Set realistic goals (e.g., improve by 5 WPM each week)

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Looking at the keyboard while typing
  • Using only a few fingers instead of all ten
  • Poor posture leading to fatigue and errors
  • Forgetting to return to home position
  • Being discouraged by initial slow speed

Recommended Practice Schedule

Week Focus Area Goal
1 Home row mastery Touch typing without looking
2 Top and bottom rows Comfortable with all alphabet keys
3 Numbers and symbols 15-20 WPM with basic text
4 Full sentences 25-30 WPM with good accuracy
